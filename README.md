E-Commerce Analytics Project

Project Overview
This project analyzes e-commerce transactional data to generate actionable business insights using SQL, Excel, and Power BI.
The objective was to transform raw transactional data into structured KPIs and interactive dashboards that support data-driven decision-making across sales, seller performance, customer behavior, delivery efficiency, and payment analysis.

Business Objectives
Analyze overall revenue performance and growth trends
Identify top-performing sellers and revenue concentration
Understand customer purchasing behavior and repeat purchase rate
Measure delivery efficiency and its impact on customer reviews
Analyze review distribution and customer satisfaction levels
Evaluate payment method usage and installment behavior

Dataset Description
Total Orders: 100K+
Total Customers: 90K+
Total Sellers: 3K+
Total Products: 30K+
Time Period Covered: Multi-year transactional dataset

Implemented calculated columns and DAX measures
Managed disconnected tables (geolocation and category translation)
Optimized model performance by removing redundant columns
Ensured appropriate cardinality and filter direction

Tools and Technologies Used:
PostgreSQL (Data Extraction and Querying)
SQL (Joins, Aggregations, CTEs, Window Functions)
Excel (Data Cleaning and Validation)
Power BI (Data Modeling and Dashboard Development)
DAX (KPI and Advanced Measures)
GitHub (Version Control and Documentation)

Dashboard Structure:
Executive Overview
Seller Performance
Customer and Delivery Insights
Repeat Customer Percentage
Key DAX Measures

Key Insights:
Revenue peaks during festive and promotional periods.
A small percentage of sellers contribute a large share of total revenue.
Late deliveries significantly reduce customer review scores.
Credit card is the most preferred payment method.
Higher installment usage correlates with higher order value.
Certain states dominate overall revenue contribution.

Challenges and Solutions:
Managed many-to-many relationships between orders and products.
Cleaned inconsistent state naming conventions.
Calculated delivery time using date difference logic.
Handled null review scores and cancelled orders.
Improved dashboard performance by optimizing data model.

Business Impact:
Identified high-revenue regions for targeted marketing strategies.
Highlighted sellers generating high revenue but low customer ratings.
Detected operational inefficiencies related to delivery delays.
Enabled interactive KPI tracking for better business decis
